12 There are a number of keys on our keyboards that we usually hold in combination
13 with others. We also have keys that we usually press and release, and don't want
14 to hold them for repeating. Then we have the others which we sometimes hold to
15 repeat them, such as `Space` held to insert a number of whitespace characters.
16 The interesting thing that falls out of this, is that there are keys we hold,
17 but never tap, and others which we never hold. What if we could combine these?
18 
19 What if we had a key that acted as `Control` when held, but `Esc` when tapped in
20 isolation? No useful functionality lost, and we have one key less to place on
21 the keyboard! We can even put `Esc` to a more convenient position, perhaps.
22 
23 Dual-use keys do just this: if you hold them, and press any other key, they will
24 act as a modifier or momentary layer switcher. If you hold them for a longer
25 period, they - again - will act as modifiers / momentary switchers. But if you
26 tap and release them in isolation, they will act as another key instead.

28 ## Using the plugin
29 
30 ```c++
31 #include <Kaleidoscope.h>
32 #include <Kaleidoscope-DualUse.h>
33 
34 // in the keymap:
35 CTL_T(Escape), LT(_LAYER, Escape)

37 void setup(void) {
38  Kaleidoscope.use(&DualUse);
39 
40  Kaleidoscope.setup();
41 }
42 ```

44 ## Keymap markup
45 
46 The plugin provides a number of macros one can use in keymap definitions:
47 
48 ### `CTL_T(key)`
49 
50 > A key that acts as the *left* `Control` when held, or used in conjunction with
51 > other keys, but as `key` when tapped in isolation. The `key` argument must be
52 > a plain old key, and can't have any modifiers or anything else applied.
53 
54 ### `ALT_T(key)`
55 
56 > A key that acts as the *left* `Alt` when held, or used in conjunction with
57 > other keys, but as `key` when tapped in isolation. The `key` argument must be
58 > a plain old key, and can't have any modifiers or anything else applied.
59 
60 ### `SFT_T(key)`
61 
62 > A key that acts as the *left* `Shift` when held, or used in conjunction with
63 > other keys, but as `key` when tapped in isolation. The `key` argument must be
64 > a plain old key, and can't have any modifiers or anything else applied.
65 
66 ### `GUI_T(key)`
67 
68 > A key that acts as the *left* `GUI` when held, or used in conjunction with
69 > other keys, but as `key` when tapped in isolation. The `key` argument must be
70 > a plain old key, and can't have any modifiers or anything else applied.
71 
72 ### `MT(mod, key)`
73 
74 > A key that acts as `mod` when held, or used in conjunction with other keys,
75 > but as `key` when tapped in isolation. The `key` argument must be a plain old
76 > key, and can't have any modifiers or anything else applied. The `mod` argument
77 > can be any of the modifiers, *left* or *right* alike.
78 
79 ### `LT(layer, key)`
80 
81 > A key that momentarily switches to `layer` when held, or used in conjunction
82 > with other keys, but as `key` when tapped in isolation. The `key` argument
83 > must be a plain old key, and can't have any modifiers or anything else
84 > applied.

86 ## Plugin methods
87 
88 The plugin provides a single object, `DualUse`, with the following property:
89 
90 ### `.time_out`
91 
92 > The number of milliseconds to wait before considering a held key in isolation
93 > as its secondary role. That is, we'd have to hold a `Z/Control` key this long,
94 > by itself, to trigger the `Control` role.
95 >
96 > Defaults to 1000.
